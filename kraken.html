Release the Kraken!

In mari multa latent or “In the ocean, many things are hidden.” Lurking in the depths of the ocean is the Kraken. As a child, you were probably told the story of how Perseus, in his valiant effort to save Andromeda, had to defeat the Kraken. For reasons that we won’t delve into here, Poseidon had sent the Kraken to ravage the kingdom of Ethiopia. To appease the angry god and save his kingdom, Andromeda’s father had to sacrifice her, his only daughter, to the monster. He chained her to a cliff, where she waited to be devoured by the Kraken. But Perseus came to her rescue and just at the moment that the Kraken opened its mouth wide, Perseus sprang into the air and drove his sword deep into the monster’s throat. The Kraken bellowed, lashed its tail wildly, and rolled over on its back. It sank and the sea was tinted red by its blood. Ever since, that stretch of water has been called the Red Sea.
